1. Meeting opener: the two-minute explanation

What are we building?
We are building Shearwater's digital operating headquarters: one secure platform connecting internal teams, travel agents and customers to the same trusted information, workflows and booking ecosystem. Each group receives a portal designed for its responsibilities. Harold is the shared AI assistant that helps users understand information and complete approved work, while always respecting their permissions.

The platform is not three unrelated websites and it is not simply a chatbot. It is one operational system with three controlled experiences:

  • Team Access helps employees run the company.
  • Agent Access helps travel partners search, quote, book and manage their customers.
  • Customer Access helps guests discover, book, manage and communicate about their experience.
  • Harold assists all three groups using the information and actions each user is allowed to access.

The first build priority is the shared foundation and Team Access. Agent and Customer Access should only be implemented after identity, permissions, organizational structure and core operational data are reliable.

What success looks like

  • One source of truth for users, customers, agents, products, availability, rates and bookings.
  • Fewer manual handovers, duplicated spreadsheets and disconnected conversations.
  • Clear ownership and audit history for operational work.
  • Faster service for agents and guests without exposing internal or confidential information.
  • AI that assists real workflows rather than acting as an isolated novelty.

All portals use shared platform services. The user interface changes by audience, but the underlying customer, availability, rate and booking records should not be duplicated. Integrations are adapters to external systems; they should not leak directly into every screen.

Core architectural principle

One platform, many access contexts. Authentication proves who the person is. Membership determines which organization or customer account they belong to. Roles and permissions determine what they may see or do. Portal selection only changes the experience; it does not replace authorization.

Agent onboarding

Agent registration should not automatically grant commercial access. The recommended flow is application or invitation, agency verification, agreement and rate assignment, user invitation, then activation. Each agency should have its own administrators who can manage approved colleagues within limits.

Example Agent Access flow

  1. The agent signs in and the platform identifies their agency and applicable contract.
  2. The agent searches live availability for a customer's dates.
  3. The pricing service returns only eligible rates and conditions.
  4. Harold helps compare options or prepare a quote.
  5. The agent creates an enquiry, provisional reservation or booking according to agreed rules.
  6. Shearwater staff are notified only when review or action is required.

7. Harold: shared intelligence, controlled execution

Harold is a capability layer across the platform, not a separate source of truth. The database and connected operational systems remain authoritative.

Harold's four operating modes

ModeExamplesControl
AnswerFind a policy, explain a product, summarize a booking or answer a rate question.Retrieve only approved sources visible to the current user and show source context where useful.
DraftPrepare an email, quotation explanation, meeting summary or customer response.A human reviews before external transmission unless an exact low-risk template is approved.
RecommendSuggest an upsell, next task, account follow-up or operational priority.Clearly label recommendations; do not present them as confirmed decisions.
ActCreate a task, schedule a meeting, update permitted CRM fields or initiate an approved workflow.Permission check, confirmation for meaningful changes, idempotency and audit record are mandatory.

Actions Harold should never perform autonomously at first

  • Confirm unavailable inventory or override the booking source of truth.
  • Change contractual rates, discounts, refunds or cancellation penalties.
  • Send sensitive or high-impact messages without review.
  • Expose internal notes, personal data or information outside the user's membership.
  • Delete business records or conceal audit history.
  • Provide invented safety, medical, legal or payment guidance.

Human handover contract

A handover should include the user, access context, conversation summary, relevant booking or customer, reason for escalation, urgency, suggested next action and full transcript access for the assigned team. Ownership must visibly change from AI to a named human or queue.

Design rule: AI confidence is not authorization. Even when Harold is confident, the user, role, portal and approval policy still determine whether an action is allowed.

Recommended technology direction

Frontend: React, TypeScript and TailwindCSS with portal-specific feature modules and a shared design system.

Initial backend: Supabase for managed PostgreSQL, authentication, Row-Level Security, Storage, Realtime and server-side functions. It is suitable for the foundation if migrations and security policies are treated as production code.

Later service layer: Add a dedicated TypeScript API or worker service when integrations, payments, reservations or AI workflows require long-running jobs, queues, stricter orchestration or vendor isolation. This can be added without replacing PostgreSQL or authentication.

Source-of-truth rule

Each important field must have an owner. For example, if Activitar owns booking confirmation and inventory, Shearwater may mirror those records but must not silently contradict them. The platform should store external IDs, synchronization status, source timestamps and error details.

10. Unified authentication and authorization

  1. The user opens one branded authentication experience.
  2. Supabase verifies the identity and returns a secure session.
  3. The platform loads active access memberships.
  4. If only one context exists, the user is routed to it. If several exist, a workspace switcher is shown.
  5. Every data request is checked again at database or server level using membership and permission rules.
Important: Hiding a navigation item is not security. Portal routes, API operations, database rows, files and AI tools must all enforce the same authorization policy.

Minimum security controls

  • Row-Level Security on every exposed company or customer table.
  • Organization identifier on every organization-owned record.
  • MFA for administrators and selected high-risk Team roles.
  • Service credentials restricted to trusted server environments.
  • Invitation-only onboarding for Team and Agent Access.
  • Email verification, reset-password flow, session revocation and disabled-account handling.
  • Consent, retention and access policies for customer data and AI conversations.
  • Audit logging for access changes, commercial changes and AI actions.

11. Integration model

Activitar

The current vision identifies Activitar as a source of imported orders and potentially availability. Before development, the business must decide whether Activitar is authoritative for products, inventory, prices, reservations, confirmations and payments individually. The answer may differ by capability.

Integration pattern

  1. Receive data through a documented API, webhook or scheduled synchronization.
  2. Validate and map external identifiers to internal records.
  3. Process the message idempotently so retries cannot create duplicates.
  4. Store source, timestamp, status and raw failure context safely.
  5. Publish a platform event for notifications, CRM and operational tasks.
  6. Provide reconciliation tools for unmatched or failed records.

Other likely integrations

  • Email provider for invitations, notifications and controlled outbound messages.
  • Website chat and customer entry points.
  • Calendar and meeting provider if required.
  • Payment provider only after booking ownership and financial workflows are agreed.
  • AI model provider behind a Shearwater-controlled orchestration and audit layer.

What should not happen

  • Do not build all portal screens before the shared data and permission model.
  • Do not let each portal create a separate customer or booking database.
  • Do not connect Harold directly to unrestricted database credentials.
  • Do not make direct booking assumptions before Activitar ownership is agreed.
  • Do not treat the current dashboard mock data as the production domain model.

15. Developer definition of done

A feature is not complete merely because the screen exists. For this platform, definition of done includes:

  • Real database records and migrations, with typed application interfaces.
  • Authentication, authorization and Row-Level Security tests.
  • Loading, empty, error, offline or retry behavior where relevant.
  • Audit history for sensitive actions.
  • Accessible and responsive user experience.
  • Integration idempotency and reconciliation for external records.
  • Monitoring, structured errors and support diagnostics.
  • Acceptance criteria demonstrated using realistic role-based scenarios.

16. Closing statement

The platform's value will come from connecting people and workflows around trusted operational data. Team, Agent and Customer Access are different experiences, but they must share one identity model, one permission system and one set of business records. Harold should make that platform easier to use; Harold should never become a shortcut around its security or business rules.
